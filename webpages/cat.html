Four Weeks to One Year

Juvenile Kitten üêæ

Kittens can start being introduced to soft wet kitten food typically around three to four weeks after birth. According to the ASPCA, kittens at this age should eat half to one cup of dry kitten food or six to nine ounces of wet kitten food per day. If your kitten has difficulties eating hard food, a small amount of water can be added to soften the food. Cat food that is optimized for kittens provides the additional nutrients that are needed for growth, energy, and wellness. Depending on the breed, your cat may have different dietary requirements. You should always consult with your veterinarian for recommendations.

One Year to Seven Years

Adult Cat üêà

This age is when kittens reach the cat stage and do not need as many nutrients. At this age, their level of activity decreases, and so does their metabolism. It is not recommended to leave food out for the cats all day. Instead, provide food a couple times a day so they eat meals rather than snacking throughout the day. This practice reduces the risk of obesity and other weight-related feline ailments.

FAQ for Cats!

How can I tell if my cat is happy?

Cats are very good at showing emotions! Their happiness is represented through purring, kneading, slow blinking, and a relaxed body posture.

How often should I feed my cat?

Adult cats should be fed twice a day, while kittens should be fed three to four times a day.

Why do cats love boxes?

In the wild, cats are ambush predators (scary!). This means that they catch their prey by stealthily stalking it and then pouncing when the moment is just right.

A box is the perfect hiding spot to hide while waiting to ambush their prey. They also provide safety!

Why does a cat's tongue have a rough texture?

Have you ever let your cat lick you? If so, you know that the feeling is weird and sharp.
That's because a cat's tongue is covered in tiny, hook-like structures called papillae. These aids the cat in grooming and help them eat meat and lap milk.

Can spaying or neutering your cat prolong its life?

Yes! It also have other benefits such as preventing reproductive cancer, less aggressive behaviour, lower risk of infections, and much more!
